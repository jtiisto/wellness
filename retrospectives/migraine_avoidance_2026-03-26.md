# Migraine Avoidance Retrospective — 2026-03-26

**Context:** High migraine risk day with no migraine outcome. Quick analysis to identify protective factors.

---

## Part 1: Likely Protective Factors (Choose 2-3 that resonate)

Ask yourself: "Which of these might have made the difference today?"

- **Sleep quality/quantity** — Did you get sufficient, quality sleep last night?
- **Hydration** — Were you consistently hydrated throughout the day?
- **Stress management** — Did you actively reduce stress (breathing, breaks, planning)?
- **Caffeine timing/amount** — Did you avoid excess caffeine or time it well?
- **Movement/exercise** — Did you move regularly or exercise (even light activity)?
- **Meal timing/quality** — Did you eat regular, balanced meals (avoid skipping)?
- **Screen breaks** — Did you take frequent visual breaks?
- **Avoided triggers** — Did you consciously avoid known migraine triggers (foods, smells, sounds)?
- **Weather adaptation** — Did weather changes feel less impactful than usual?

---

## Part 2: Reflection Questions (5-7 minutes)

Go through these quickly; jot brief notes:

1. **Sleep & Recovery**
   - How many hours did you sleep last night? How was the quality?
   - Did you feel rested when you woke up?

2. **Hydration & Nutrition**
   - Did you drink water consistently? Estimate intake.
   - Did you skip any meals or have irregular eating?

3. **Stress & Mental State**
   - What was your stress level (1-10) during the day?
   - Did you do anything intentionally calming (walk, meditation, deep breathing)?

4. **Physical Activity**
   - Did you exercise or move regularly today?
   - How did your body feel (tense, relaxed, energized)?

5. **Sensory & Environmental**
   - Any unusual screen time or bright light exposure?
   - Weather changes (barometric pressure, temperature)?
   - Were you exposed to known triggers (certain foods, strong smells)?

6. **Medication/Supplements**
   - Did you take any preventive medication or supplements today?
   - Any changes to your routine?

7. **Overall Reflection**
   - If you had to pick ONE thing that helped most, what would it be?
   - What felt different about today compared to other high-risk days?

---

## Part 3: Data to Track Going Forward

Prioritize capturing these for pattern recognition:

**High value (daily tracking):**
- Sleep duration & quality (1-5 scale)
- Hydration level (cups of water or 1-5 scale)
- Stress level (1-10)
- Whether migraine occurred (yes/no)

**Medium value (when relevant):**
- Exercise/movement (yes/no, type, duration)
- Significant weather changes (note them)
- Major stressors or positive events
- Notable dietary changes or skipped meals

**Optional (if you notice patterns):**
- Caffeine intake timing
- Screen time duration
- Menstrual cycle phase (if applicable)
- Barometric pressure
