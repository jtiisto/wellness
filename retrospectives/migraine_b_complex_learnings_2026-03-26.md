## Hypotheses to Test With Blood Work

### H1: B-Complex is causing overmethylation that lowers migraine threshold [Confidence: 0.6]
**Theory:** Folate was already adequate (17.5 ng/mL) before supplementation. Adding 665 mcg of L-5-MTHF daily on top of adequate stores may push the methylation cycle harder than needed, increasing neurological excitability and/or histamine levels — both known migraine contributors.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Skipping B-Complex coincided with lowest stress day (24) and no migraine despite multiple triggers
- B2 (400 mg) taken alone provided migraine protection without the methylation push
- Mechanism is well-documented in methylation literature (overmethylation -> histamine -> migraine)

**What would strengthen this hypothesis:**
- Blood work shows B12 and/or folate are now well above optimal (overshoot)
- Homocysteine has already normalized (meaning the B-Complex did its job and may no longer be needed)
- MCV has reversed its upward trend

**What would weaken this hypothesis:**
- Homocysteine still elevated despite 4 months of supplementation (still need the methyl support)
- B12 still in low-normal range (not over-supplementing)
- This was simply a lucky day — one data point is not a pattern

### H2: B2 alone is sufficient migraine protection; B-Complex adds no migraine benefit and may net-negative [Confidence: 0.5]
**Theory:** The standalone B2 at 400 mg handles migraine prophylaxis. The B-Complex was started for homocysteine/methylation reasons, not migraine. If its methylation effects are counteracting the B2's protective effect, the net result is worse migraine management.

**What would support action on this:**
- Homocysteine improved -> B-Complex served its purpose
- Can switch to targeted, non-methylated B12 (hydroxocobalamin) to maintain B12 support without methylation overshoot
- Pattern holds over multiple B-Complex skip days

### H3: Lower GLP-1 dose contributed to migraine avoidance [Confidence: 0.4]
**Theory:** Slightly lower GLP-1 dose in second week may reduce subclinical GI stress or shift glucose dynamics in a way that reduces migraine susceptibility. Possible mechanisms: less gastroparesis effect during fasting (delayed lunch was handled better), reduced nausea signaling.

**Hard to isolate:** GLP-1 dose changes are slow and overlapping with other variables. Lower priority for investigation.

### H4: Deep sleep quality is a stronger migraine predictor than total sleep [Confidence: 0.5]
**Theory:** 1.55h deep sleep (best in 5 days) provided meaningful neural recovery despite only 6.57h total sleep. Deep sleep may matter more than total hours for migraine threshold.

**Worth tracking:** Compare deep sleep hours on future migraine vs no-migraine days.

## Questions for Blood Draw Results

1. **Homocysteine:** Has it come down from 11.4? Target is 5-9 umol/L.
   - If yes -> B-Complex has done its job; consider changing formulation
   - If no -> may need higher B12 dose or different approach regardless
2. **B12:** Where is it now after ~4 months of 250 mcg methylcobalamin?
   - If >500-600 -> adequate, original deficiency addressed
   - If >1000 -> possible overshoot
   - If still <500 -> 250 mcg wasn't enough; need higher dose
3. **Folate / RBC Folate (if on panel):** Confirm whether methylfolate supplementation has pushed levels unnecessarily high
4. **MCV:** Has upward trend (94.8 -> 96.6) reversed?
   - If dropping -> B-vitamin status improving
   - If still rising -> supplementation not addressing the root cause

## Proposed Decision Framework (Post Blood Draw)

### If homocysteine improved AND B12 adequate:
- **Stop methylated B-Complex**
- **Continue B2 400 mg** (migraine prophylaxis)
- **Consider adding hydroxocobalamin 1000 mcg** (non-methylated B12 to maintain levels without methylation push)
- Track stress averages and migraine outcomes for 2-4 weeks to confirm improvement
- Recheck homocysteine in 3 months to ensure it stays controlled

### If homocysteine still elevated:
- B-Complex formulation may not be the right approach (B12 dose too low at 250 mcg)
- Switch to **hydroxocobalamin 1000 mcg** + keep monitoring
- Still consider dropping methylfolate given adequate baseline folate
- Investigate other causes: alcohol intake, kidney function, medication interactions

### If B12 is now very high (>1000) with homocysteine still elevated:
- Suggests absorption or utilization issue, not just intake
- Would need further workup (MMA, functional B12 markers)
